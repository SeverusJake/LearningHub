# 07 — KDP Books

**Model:** Medium-content, niche nonfiction published on Amazon KDP — workbooks with real exercises (habit trackers, finance workbooks, skill-practice books), niche how-to ebooks (30-60 pages solving one specific problem), and log books with genuinely useful structure; this is explicitly not low-content spam (blank journals, generic planners, one-word-different notebooks) — that market is saturated and Amazon now quality-gates it hard · **First $ (typical):** 4-8wks · **Ceiling:** $50-500/mo · **Payout:** bank transfer / Payoneer (NOT PayPal)

**AI does:** niche research, outlines, full manuscript drafts, a self-edit pass, cover concepts, and metadata (title/subtitle, keywords, categories) · **You do:** create the KDP account and complete the tax interview, fact-check and voice-pass every manuscript against the quality gates, publish with the honest AI-disclosure answer, and monitor payout

## Honest expectations

Most first books sell close to zero copies in their first few weeks — KDP's search and recommendation algorithms need sales history and reviews before they'll surface a new title to anyone who isn't already searching for it by near-exact keyword, and this folder runs with no ad budget by default, so a book with no organic pull just sits on page 5. Niche selection decides roughly 80% of the outcome before a single word of the manuscript gets written: a book solving one specific, currently-underserved problem for a specific audience (a workbook for a niche certification, a log book for a specific hobby's specific tracking need) will outsell a well-written book in a crowded, vague niche (generic "productivity" or "mindfulness") almost every time. This is the slowest folder in the track to produce a first dollar — 4-8 weeks is typical, and that's measured from when a book actually goes live, not from when you start writing it — but it's also the most durable: a published book keeps earning passively for years with no further work, and a catalog of 10+ titles compounds in a way none of this track's single-instance products do. Judge this folder by the catalog at the 12-week mark, not any one book's first two weeks — a single title's early numbers are close to noise.

## Rules and compliance

**This is the strictest disclosure requirement in the track — read this in full before publishing a single book.**

- **KDP asks directly, at publish time, whether your content is AI-generated. Answer honestly, every time, for every book.** As of this writing, Amazon permits AI-generated content on KDP as long as it's disclosed through this question — undisclosed AI content that should have been disclosed is a policy violation that can get a title or an entire account suspended, not just the one book pulled.
- **"AI-generated" and "AI-assisted" are different KDP categories with different disclosure rules — know which one your actual process is, and confirm the current definitions before every single publish, not just your first one.** Amazon has changed this wording and this boundary before, and can change it again. Read KDP's own current content-guidelines page (the "AI content" section at kdp.amazon.com) immediately before each publish — don't rely on this document's summary, or last month's understanding, to answer the publish-time question.
- **Every book is fact-checked by you personally before it goes live, cover to cover, no exceptions.** This is not a skim — verify every factual claim, every number, every named technique or reference the manuscript makes, especially in workbooks and how-to books where a wrong instruction can actually hurt a reader's outcome (a finance workbook with a wrong tax figure, a how-to book with an unsafe technique).
- **No keyword-stuffed titles or subtitles.** KDP's own terms of service prohibit cramming search terms into the title/subtitle field that don't accurately describe the book. Search terms belong in the 7 backend keyword fields, not the title.
- **No copyrighted material.** No lifted text, no traced diagrams, no reproduced exercises or frameworks from another published book, no using a named branded framework or method you don't hold rights to reference commercially.

Full compliance floor (applies to every folder in this track, no exceptions):

- No fake reviews.
- No account misrepresentation or sharing.
- No spam.
- No undisclosed AI where a platform requires disclosure.
- No trademark/IP infringement.
- No fabricated credentials.
- Income figures are ranges with no guarantee.
- Taxes are the learner's own responsibility (this is not tax advice).

## Eligibility check

Do this before producing a single manuscript — if payout isn't confirmed, everything after this is wasted effort.

1. Create a KDP account at kdp.amazon.com (uses your existing Amazon account, or a new one).
2. Complete KDP's tax interview in full. Non-US learners fill out a W-8BEN (or W-8BEN-E) through KDP's own interview flow — this sets your US tax withholding rate on royalties and has to be done before any title can go live for sale.
3. In KDP's payment/royalties settings, confirm your payout method. **This folder pays out via direct bank transfer or Payoneer — it does NOT support PayPal, unlike every other folder in this track.** Confirm your country is on Amazon's current supported-countries list for direct bank deposit, or set up a free Payoneer account (payoneer.com) as the alternative if direct bank transfer isn't available where you are.
4. Note KDP's current royalty payout schedule and minimum threshold from KDP's own payments page (royalties typically pay out roughly 60 days after the month they're earned, and there's a minimum balance that triggers a payment) — don't assume last year's numbers still hold; check the live page.
5. Only after the tax interview is complete and a payout method is confirmed live, move to Phase 1 of the playbook.

## Kill / scale criteria

**Kill this folder** if, after publishing 5 quality books (each one passing every gate in PLAYBOOK.md's Phase 2) and 12 weeks of them being live, combined revenue across the whole catalog is still under $20/mo. That's a real signal on niche selection or execution, not a reason to grind out a sixth book on the same pattern. Bank the niche-research lessons and put weekend time elsewhere.

**Scale a winning niche** the moment any single title shows repeat weekly sales, not one fluke: build out a series in that niche (a volume 2, a companion workbook, a different skill level of the same topic), publish every title in both paperback and ebook formats, add KDP's A+ content module to the winners, and run free-promo days within KDP Select's terms to earn organic reviews — never buy or exchange reviews, ever.
