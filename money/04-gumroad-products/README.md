# 04 — Gumroad Products

**Model:** Digital products sold on Gumroad — dev-focused Notion templates, code boilerplates, and PDF cheatsheet packs, built once and sold repeatedly with no per-sale fulfillment work · **First $ (typical):** 2-6wks · **Ceiling:** $50-500/mo · **Payout:** PayPal

**AI does:** the product content itself (Notion page structures, boilerplate repos, cheatsheet copy), listing titles and descriptions, cover-art prompts, and distribution-post drafts · **You do:** create the Gumroad account, connect PayPal, build the Notion pages from spec, upload and publish each product, post the distribution drafts, and answer buyer questions

## Honest expectations

A Gumroad product does not sell itself the moment it's published — Gumroad's own discovery surface sends you close to zero traffic, so every sale traces back to a specific distribution push you made: a Reddit comment, an X thread, a dev.to post, an email you sent. Build a free or pay-what-you-want "lite" version of at least one product early — it costs nothing to give away, and wired through Gumroad's follow feature it builds an email list that becomes the fastest channel for selling every paid product that comes after it. Expect your first 2-3 products to mostly teach you which distribution channel and which format your specific audience responds to, not to produce meaningful income — the real money shows up once you know that and repeat it. The three product lines here (Notion templates, boilerplates, PDF packs) take different build effort and sell through different channels, so don't commit to one exclusively until you've shipped at least one of each. Treat your first $20-30 as validation that a specific product/channel pairing works, not as a stable baseline to project forward.

## Rules and compliance

Every sale here runs through Gumroad, so you're bound by Gumroad's Acceptable Use Policy and Terms of Service: no reselling content you don't hold the rights to, no plagiarized or scraped material — this includes cheatsheet content lifted from documentation, blog posts, or forum answers without rewriting it in your own words and structure — no counterfeit or pirated software, and nothing from Gumroad's prohibited-items list. Each product line has a specific originality bar. Notion templates must be your own page structures, not a copy of someone else's published template. Boilerplates must be code you wrote or assembled from permissively licensed open-source pieces (MIT/Apache/BSD) with attribution kept intact in the README — never someone else's paid starter repackaged. PDF cheatsheets must be your own explanations in your own words, even for well-documented topics like kubectl commands or POSIX one-liners — re-explaining a command in your own structure is fine, copying a man page or a blog's exact wording is not. Listing descriptions have to describe exactly what's in the download: if the Notion template has 12 pages, say 12, not "dozens"; if the boilerplate doesn't include a live demo, don't imply it does.

Full compliance floor (applies to every folder in this track, no exceptions):

- No fake reviews.
- No account misrepresentation or sharing.
- No spam.
- No undisclosed AI where a platform requires disclosure.
- No trademark/IP infringement.
- No fabricated credentials.
- Income figures are ranges with no guarantee.
- Taxes are the learner's own responsibility (this is not tax advice).

## Eligibility check

Do this before building anything — if payout doesn't work for your country, everything after this is wasted effort.

1. Create a Gumroad account (gumroad.com) and complete their creator profile.
2. In Gumroad's Settings → Payments, add PayPal as your payout method and confirm PayPal payouts are actually enabled for your country — Gumroad pays via PayPal in most but not all supported countries, so check this before writing any product content, not after your first sale.
3. Check Gumroad's minimum payout threshold and payout schedule in the same settings page, and add the basic tax information Gumroad requires before payouts clear — this is an account step to unblock getting paid, not tax advice.
4. Only after PayPal payout is confirmed working, move to Phase 1 of the playbook.

## Kill / scale criteria

**Kill a product line** — not a single product, the whole line: Notion templates, boilerplates, or PDF packs — if it's under $20 total revenue after 8 weeks and at least 4 distinct distribution pushes (4 separate posts, threads, or emails, not the same post edited and reposted). At that point stop building new products in that line and put the build time into a line that's showing signal.

**Scale a winner** — any single product with real repeat sales, not just a launch-day spike — by bundling it with 1-2 related products at a discount, adding a tiered version (a $9 basic tier next to a $19-29 "pro" tier with extra templates, roles, or sheets), and pushing it to the email list built through the free-lite funnel. A winner here is also worth cross-promoting from `03-micro-tools/` if a tool in that folder shares the same audience — a Kubernetes-focused micro-tool and the k8s troubleshooting cheatsheet, for example.
