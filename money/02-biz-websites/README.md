# 02 — Biz Websites

**Model:** Cold-outreach web design for local plumbers (one vertical, chosen up front) — $200-500 flat per site + optional $20-30/mo care plan · **First $ (typical):** 2-4wks · **Ceiling:** $400-2000/mo · **Payout:** PayPal invoice

**AI does:** vertical/city research, 3 portfolio demo sites, the 3-email outreach sequence, proposal PDFs, and the actual site builds · **You do:** buy the portfolio domain, set up PayPal invoicing, send every outreach email yourself, take client calls, review each build before it ships, and log everything in the tracker

## Honest expectations

This is a sales job wearing a web-design costume — the build is the easy 10% of the work, and the other 90% is finding plumbers with bad websites and convincing a stranger to reply to your email. Cold email to small local businesses typically runs a 1-3% reply rate, and only a fraction of replies become paying clients, so plan on roughly 100 sends producing 1-2 clients, not 10. Expect long silence: most emails get no response at all, and that's normal, not proof your pitch is broken. The site itself — a fast, static, mobile-first 1-3 page build — takes AI minutes to generate and you under a day to review and ship; the weeks-long part is the outreach grind and the sales conversation that gets someone to say yes and pay a deposit. Treat every send as one input into a numbers game, not a referendum on your skill.

## Rules and compliance

CAN-SPAM basics apply to every email, no exceptions: send from your real name and a real reply-to address (no spoofed headers or fake sender names), include a working opt-out (a plain "reply STOP to opt out" is sufficient at this volume, and you must actually honor it), and never use a deceptive or clickbait subject line — the subject has to describe what's actually in the email. This is targeted, researched, low-volume outreach to specific businesses you personally looked at — never a scraped-list blast to hundreds of unverified addresses. If you haven't personally checked the business's own site or listing before emailing them, don't send it. Some places layer stricter rules on top of CAN-SPAM (e.g., Canada's CASL requires opt-in consent, not just opt-out) — if you target businesses outside the US, check your target country's or state's equivalent law first. Whatever you sell in the proposal is exactly what gets delivered: same page count, same features, same timeline, no quietly shrinking scope after the deposit clears.

## Eligibility check

1. Confirm PayPal can send invoices on your account (paypal.com → upgrade to or confirm a PayPal Business account) — you need this for the 50% deposit and the final payment.
2. Confirm you can attach or reference a PDF proposal alongside a PayPal invoice.
3. Decide upfront who pays for each client's live domain and hosting: either pass the ~$10-15/yr cost to the client (cleanest, protects your margin) or use the client's own existing domain/hosting if they already have one — never buy or hold a client's production domain under your own name.
4. Your one portfolio domain (~$10) is separate from any client domain — it's the demo site you link to in outreach, and it stays yours.

Only move to Phase 2 outreach once PayPal invoicing is confirmed working end to end.

## Kill / scale criteria

Kill this vertical after 150 researched, individually-checked contacts with 0 closed deals — that's the bar, not "it feels slow." At that point either switch verticals (electricians, landscapers, dentists all run the same playbook) or shelve the folder and reallocate your time elsewhere. Scale by niching harder (e.g., narrow "plumbers" down to "emergency-call plumbers in three specific suburbs"), asking every delivered client for one referral at handoff, and stacking $20-30/mo care-plan revenue on top of flat-fee builds — five care-plan clients alone is $100-150/mo of recurring income before you've sold a single new site that month.
