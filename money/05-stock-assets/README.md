# 05 — Stock Assets

**Model:** Generate GenAI images and vectors, label them as AI-generated, and sell them as royalty-free stock through Adobe Stock's contributor marketplace · **First $ (typical):** 3-8wks · **Ceiling:** $20-300/mo · **Payout:** PayPal

**AI does:** prompt generation, batch image/vector generation, upscaling, and title + keyword metadata writing · **You do:** create and verify the contributor account, curate every batch against the quality gate, apply the GenAI label, upload, and track what sells

## Honest expectations

Acceptance rates commonly run 30-70% while you're still learning where Adobe's review bar sits — expect early batches to get rejected for reasons that feel arbitrary until a pattern emerges (similar-to-existing-content, compositional, and technical flags are the usual culprits). Per-asset earnings are small: think cents to a few dollars per download, not a payday per image, and the actual share you get depends on your contributor level and the license type the buyer picks. Volume and niche selection decide the outcome far more than any single great image — a portfolio of 500+ well-targeted assets in commercially useful niches will outearn 50 beautiful ones nobody searches for. Say this plainly: this is the most passive folder in the track once assets are live — no client chasing, no launch posts, no ongoing promotion — but it is also the slowest-compounding one here. Earnings trickle in per-download over months and years rather than arriving in a burst, and the catalog only starts producing meaningful income once it is genuinely large.

## Rules and compliance

This folder has a stricter compliance floor than the rest of the track, because Adobe Stock enforces it at the platform level and getting it wrong risks your contributor account, not just one asset:

- **Label every GenAI submission at upload time, always.** Adobe requires you to mark AI-generated content as such in its contributor portal at the point of submission — there is no "forget it once and fix it later," and no exception for an asset that "looks real enough" not to need it.
- **No recognizable people or faces, ever — not even AI-generated ones.** Even a fully synthetic face that resembles no real person carries model-release complications the review process isn't built to clear for GenAI content. The simple rule: keep faces and recognizable human likenesses out of every prompt entirely. If a scene needs a human element, use silhouettes, hands-only crops with no distinguishing features, or leave people out of the frame altogether.
- **No logos, trademarks, or artist-style-mimicry prompts.** Never prompt "in the style of [artist/brand]," never generate anything containing a recognizable logo, brand mark, or trademarked product shape, and reject any asset where the model hallucinated one in.
- **No editorial claims.** Don't caption or keyword an asset as if it depicts a real news event, a real place tied to current events, or a real identifiable person or thing — that's editorial content and it needs a different, much stricter submission path you are not using here.
- **Adobe's policy is not every platform's policy.** If you ever expand to a second stock platform, verify that platform's current GenAI content policy yourself before submitting there — Shutterstock and Getty's rules differ from Adobe's and from each other, and "it was fine on Adobe" is not a compliance check.

Full compliance floor (applies to every folder in this track, no exceptions):

- No fake reviews.
- No account misrepresentation or sharing.
- No spam.
- No undisclosed AI where a platform requires disclosure.
- No trademark/IP infringement.
- No fabricated credentials.
- Income figures are ranges with no guarantee.
- Taxes are the learner's own responsibility (this is not tax advice).

## Eligibility check

Do this before generating a single asset — if the account or payout isn't confirmed, everything after this is wasted effort.

1. Create an Adobe Stock contributor account at contributor.stock.adobe.com.
2. Complete the required tax interview in the contributor portal — non-US contributors file a W-8BEN (or W-8BEN-E for entities) to establish foreign-status withholding; US contributors file a W-9. Payouts won't release until this is on file.
3. In the contributor portal's payment settings, confirm PayPal is available and connected as your payout method, and confirm it's actually offered for your specific country — most but not all supported countries get PayPal as an option.
4. Only after the account, tax form, and confirmed payout method are all in place, move to Phase 1 of the playbook.

## Kill / scale criteria

**Kill this folder** if, after 12 weeks with 300+ assets live in the catalog, total earnings are still under $10. That's a genuine catalog-scale test, not a first-batch judgment — don't apply this bar at week 2 with 40 assets live, that's not a fair read yet.

**Scale a winning niche** — any niche where a meaningfully higher share of your assets get accepted and actually download or sell relative to your other niches — by producing roughly 10x more assets in that specific niche (more colorways, seasonal variants, and closely-related concepts) rather than spreading effort evenly across everything you've tried.
