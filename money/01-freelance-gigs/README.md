# 01 — Freelance Gigs

**Model:** Sell AI-leveraged technical services on Fiverr + Upwork — Python scraping scripts, data cleaning (CSV/Excel), Excel/Sheets automation, small bug fixes, technical writing · **First $ (typical):** days to 2 weeks · **Ceiling:** $300-2000/mo · **Payout:** PayPal

**AI does:** the actual deliverable (scraper, cleaned dataset, macro, patch, doc), gig copy, portfolio samples, and Upwork proposal drafts · **You do:** account setup, client messaging, reviewing/testing every deliverable before it ships, and sending proposals

## Honest expectations

A brand-new Fiverr or Upwork account has zero trust and zero reviews — buyers and Upwork's own ranking algorithm treat you as unproven until you prove otherwise. Expect your first 5 orders to be underpriced: you are buying reputation with cheap, fast, well-reviewed work, not maximizing dollars per hour. On Upwork specifically, response speed is the single biggest lever on your Job Success Score and search ranking — a client who gets a reply in 20 minutes will pick you over a better portfolio that replies in a day. Volume matters more than polish early on: expect 20-50 submitted proposals before your first Upwork win, because most proposals get no reply at all. None of this is fast or guaranteed — it's a numbers game you grind daily until the math starts working in your favor.

## Rules and compliance

Both platforms explicitly allow AI-assisted delivery — using Claude Code, ChatGPT, or any other tool to help write the scraper or clean the data is fine. What both platforms ban is misrepresentation: you cannot pretend a human team did work an AI did if a client asks, you cannot let someone else use your account, and you cannot share login credentials with a "team member" who is actually doing the work under your name. You are the seller of record; AI is a tool you use, the same as an IDE or a spreadsheet. Never deliver AI output you have not personally reviewed and tested — a scraper that doesn't run, or a formula that silently gives wrong numbers, is a refund and a bad review waiting to happen. Do not take client contact off-platform (email, WhatsApp, personal Skype) before an order is placed — both Fiverr and Upwork ban circumventing their messaging/payment systems pre-contract, and getting caught risks account suspension.

Full compliance floor (applies to every gig, every platform, no exceptions):

- No fake reviews.
- No account misrepresentation or sharing.
- No spam.
- No undisclosed AI where a platform requires disclosure.
- No trademark/IP infringement.
- No fabricated credentials.
- Income figures are ranges with no guarantee.
- Taxes are the learner's own responsibility (this is not tax advice).

## Eligibility check

Do this before writing a single gig description or proposal — if PayPal isn't confirmed as a valid payout method, everything after this is wasted effort.

**Fiverr:**

1. Create a Fiverr seller account (fiverr.com → Become a Seller) using your real name/identity — no aliases.
2. Complete profile verification (email, phone) when prompted.
3. Go to Settings → Billing → Payments and add PayPal as your payout method.
4. Confirm PayPal shows as "active"/"verified" in your payout settings before publishing any gig.

**Upwork:**

1. Create an Upwork freelancer account (upwork.com/signup) using your real identity.
2. Complete your profile to 100% (photo, title, overview, skills) — incomplete profiles get filtered out of search by Upwork's own algorithm.
3. Go to Settings → Get Paid → add PayPal as your payment method.
4. Confirm PayPal is listed and verified before submitting your first proposal.

Only move to Phase 1 of the playbook once both PayPal connections show verified.

## Kill / scale criteria

**Kill a niche** if it has 0 orders after 4 weeks of active listing AND you have sent 30+ Upwork proposals in that niche with no hire. That's the bar — not "it feels slow," an actual zero-orders-plus-30-proposals dead end. When a niche hits that bar, pull the Fiverr gig (or leave it live but stop promoting it) and reallocate your daily proposal volume to a niche that's showing signal.

**Scale a niche** by raising prices 25% every 5 five-star reviews. Five 5-star reviews on the $15 scraping gig → move it to ~$19. Another five → ~$24. This keeps your price climbing in step with your proof, instead of guessing at what the market will bear.
